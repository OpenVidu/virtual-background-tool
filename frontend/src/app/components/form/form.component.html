Video Conference Database Consent Form

This document explains how your data will be treated if you take part in this research study. Your participation is voluntary which means you can choose whether or not to participate. Before you make a decision you will need to know the purpose of the study and the use of your data.

If you do not understand what you are reading, do not sign it. Please ask the researcher to explain anything you do not understand, including any language contained in this form. If you decide to participate, you will be asked to sign this form and a copy will be given to you. Keep this form; in it you will find contact information and answers to questions about the study.

1 Purpose of this research

The goal of this study is to develop an application for video conferences to apply virtual background. This application can be used to distinguish the person on the video from the background scenery, so it can change the background into another image or into a blurred background like it’s currently done on some video call applications that you may know like Zoom, Microsoft Teams or Google Meet.

To do this, we need to collect a set of videos in different poses that are typical in video conference scenarios, including all the possible situations like wearing headsets or not wearing them, or talking with hand expressions or just standing.

2 Requirements for participation

In order to participate, you must be 18 years of age or older, and need to be able to follow instructions and sit still long enough for videos to be taken. You will also need a fixed camera and somewhere comfortable to sit in front of it for at least a few minutes.

3 What we need from you

If you agree to participate, we will take video portraits of you in a variety of poses. You will also be asked to provide your age, skin color, gender and hair style.

You will first be asked to take a picture of your background only, and then to be close, medium distance or far from the camera and do a set of poses with the guidance of video examples. Each video will take at least 3 seconds and some of them at least 10 seconds to get more variety of images. Then, you will be asked to repeat the same videos for the other distances and with or without headsets. The full process will take no longer than 30 minutes. There is no need to do all the videos in all the poses, but we appreciate having the full set of data.

4 Risks

Only ordinary digital photographs will be taken of you. We anticipate no risks to your health or safety.

5 Benefits

You are not expected to benefit directly from this project. However, we hope that your participation will help to improve our application technology.

6 What will happen to the information collected

6.1 Training and evaluation of a virtual background application

Your video conference videos will be used to train and test virtual background applications. This means other researchers at the Universidad Rey Juan Carlos may be allowed to have controlled access to your images. The pictures will also be run through computer programs, and may be used to train and test subject segmentation. This access will be strictly controlled and supervised.

6.2 Privacy risks

Even if some of the pictures in the final data set are inadvertently disclosed, your name will never be released. However, since the images are of your face, if any researchers inadvertently release the pictures, you may be identified. If you are uncomfortable with this possible occurrence, please do not sign this consent form or participate in the research.

Even if the demographic information was also inadvertently disclosed, the information (gender, race, and approximate age) is already clear from the photographs in most cases. Any unauthorized person who came into possession of the data would only be able to verify this information. They would not know your name or any other identifying information.

However, we will do all we can to prevent the information from being inadvertently disclosed, and we do not expect that this will happen.

6.3 Publications

Some frames of the videos could be published in journals, conference papers, magazine articles, or any investigation sites to serve as examples. Your videos could also be published along with the article as a whole dataset to serve other researchers with the same purposes.
